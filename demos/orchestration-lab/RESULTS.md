## Cross-cutting notes

**Convergence at T1 — a finding, not a shortfall.** All three modes independently arrived at the same design facts (400 respondents, 8 tasks, 2 profiles, 7 attributes, identical level counts) and none asserted "perfect balance." A light Fable lead delegating to Sonnet, a heavy Opus lead delegating to Sonnet, and a medium Codex lead working inline all land on the same numbers. The only gradient is phrasing of the honest imbalance flag. opus and 46 report the exact 1.94pp max deviation and call it negligible. fable reports a 3.08pp spread and calls Driving Time "not indicative of a randomization problem." None reproduced the reference χ²=14.6 test. When the task is computable and single-step, routing shape is a cost/latency choice, not a correctness one.

**Process divergence — how work is routed.** 46-orchestrate spawned zero subagents across all three tiers, working inline even on the judgment tier (its rule is that for compact work, briefing costs more than execution). fable-orchestrate delegated both mechanical tiers wholesale to a low-effort Sonnet (one contract each) and reserved fan-out for T3, splitting estimation to an Opus deep-reasoner and running a blind terra@xhigh Codex peer in parallel. opus-orchestrate kept the reasoning with the lead (0 delegations at T2, 0 Task spawns at T3, only the T1 script sent to Sonnet), which is why it runs longest (24.5 min / 67 turns at T3). The advisor arms are a different shape entirely. Plain solve, one out-of-band consult, one revise. The consult is the whole intervention.

**Product divergence — different answers.** (1) The estimand splits at T2. fable and opus lead with IRR-corrected AMCEs (crime −25.1pp, projoint default). 46 leads with uncorrected AMCEs (crime −16.5pp), labeled. A reader takes away 25pp from two modes and 16pp from the third. (2) 46/T3 marginal means are simply wrong (.541/.458 = 8.3pp gap vs reference .626/.374 = 25.1pp corrected / 16.5 uncorrected). 46 got the binary-invariance argument and the no-overclaim verdict right on a substantively understated number and never disclosed the IRR choice. (3) opus/T3's decorrelated peer was uncalibrated. The lead called `codex exec` without a `--model` pin, so the second opinion ran at the machine's gpt-5.5 default rather than the prescribed gpt-5.6-terra@xhigh. The memo is still the strongest inline product, so the deviation cost process fidelity, not accuracy. The figures split too. Both Codex-authored T3 figures (46, advisor-Codex) are base-R with a custom teal, off the Okabe-Ito convention every Claude-family figure keeps (dpi and caption-not-title still met).

**What each advisor consult caught.** The Claude/Fable consult verified every number against the on-disk script, table, and figure, then added the single decisive argument the solve left implicit (no baseline configuration can raise a rival's displayed AMCE above crime's point estimate, so the softening comes from sampling error, not baselines), surfaced the IRR magnitude (reporting both 16.5/25.1), and fixed a ±notation hazard, which lifted the memo to Distinction. The Codex/Sol consult caught the opposite pair. It flagged an overclaim (crime called "largest span" with 0.251 vs commute's 0.237 untested) and an over-concession (conceding the reviewer's premise when the max−min MM range is reference-invariant), softening the headline to "among the largest, comparable to commuting time."

**Cost gradient.** Within Claude Code the light-lead mode is cheaper than the heavy-lead mode and the gap widens with difficulty. fable vs opus is $1.88/$2.26 (T1), $2.27/$2.14 (T2), $4.13/$6.73 (T3). opus's own-reasoning routing spends 6.3M input tokens and 24.5 min at T3. The most expensive single cell is the T3 advisor-Claude arm at $7.08 (solve $5.52 + revise $1.57), the two-step protocol that bought the Distinction memo. Codex is off the USD scale (the CLI reports only tokens), but its footprint is a different order. It runs 67k–105k tokens per tier vs fable's 361k–875k and opus's 1.14M–6.28M "input" (Claude Code's figure includes cache reads). Within-platform ranking at the hard tier is fable ($4.13) < opus ($6.73) < advisor-Claude ($7.08). 46 and advisor-Codex sit outside the USD comparison.
