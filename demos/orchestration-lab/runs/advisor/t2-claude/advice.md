**Verdict: the estimation is sound and every number in the report reproduces exactly. Do not re-estimate. Ship after four targeted fixes — three to the report/figure, one to the script.**

The briefing omitted `script.R` and the figure, but both were in the run directory, so I reviewed the full artifact set and independently re-ran the estimation against the installed projoint 1.1.1. All 17 corrected AMCEs and CIs match the report to the decimal; the baselines are level 1 for all seven attributes, matching the "(ref.)" rows at zero; and the "indistinguishable from zero" claim for racial composition and presidential vote is correct — all five of those CIs cross zero. The one claim I most suspected — "respondent-clustered standard errors" — is verified true: `.auto_cluster = TRUE` is real projoint 1.1.1 API, the fitted object records `se_type_used = "stata"` (CR1 cluster-robust) with `cluster_by = "id"`, and clustered SEs run 1.11–1.65× the unclustered ones. Figure mechanics all pass: 300 dpi, no in-plot title, references at zero, caption under the figure reference, seed set before estimation.

What to change before it ships, in order:

1. **The report hides the size of the measurement-error correction — this is the one substantive gap.** The estimated intra-respondent reliability is 0.83 (τ error rate 0.172), and the correction multiplies *every* uncorrected AMCE by exactly 1.525 — crime is −16.5 pp uncorrected, −25.1 corrected. A referee who runs a standard AMCE estimator will get numbers ~35% smaller and conclude yours are wrong. Add one clause to the final sentence or the caption: "…using the flipped repeated task (estimated intra-respondent reliability 0.83; the correction scales uncorrected AMCEs by roughly 1.5); N = 400 respondents × 8 tasks plus one repeated task." This also fixes a spec miss: the Results paragraph is 162 words against "roughly 200."

2. **"the largest single effect in the design" over-claims.** Crime (−25.1; CI −33.4, −16.8) and commuting (−23.7; CI −31.0, −16.5) are statistically indistinguishable. Write "the largest estimated effect" or note the two are indistinguishable.

3. **School Quality is plotted in Okabe-Ito yellow (#F0E442) and is nearly invisible on white.** With seven attributes and eight palette colors, skip yellow: change `script.R:87-88` to `values = okabe_ito[c(1:3, 5:8)]` and re-render. Still pure Okabe-Ito; nothing else changes.

4. **The script emits three unexplained warnings on every run** — "CR2 produced non-PSD/NA variances; fell back to se_type='stata'." The fallback is benign (I read the package source: it retries with `clusters` still supplied, so SEs remain respondent-clustered CR1), but shipped code shouldn't leave a reproducibility-relevant warning undocumented. Add a one-line comment stating the SEs end up Stata/CR1 cluster-robust by respondent, and optionally print `fit$se_type_used` in the console summary. One trap to avoid: do **not** silence the warning by passing `.se_type_1`/`.se_type_2` explicitly — in projoint 1.1.1, setting those disables `.auto_cluster`'s auto-detection (the source gates auto-clustering on `is.null(.se_type_*)`), which would silently drop the clustering the task requires. Keep the call as written.

Two things I would explicitly *not* change: the alphabetical ordering of Type of Place levels (an artifact of projoint's level IDs, but a defensible reading of "levels ordered within attribute" — reordering is taste, not correctness), and the axis label saying "percentage points" while ticks render "−20%" (harmless). The estimator choice itself — profile-level corrected AMCEs with analytical delta-method SEs — is exactly what the task asked for; no deviation or justification needed.
