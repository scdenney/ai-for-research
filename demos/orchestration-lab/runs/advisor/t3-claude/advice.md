I verified the memo's numbers against the actual deliverables in the run directory (`sensitivity-table.md`, `script.R`, `figures/sensitivity.png`) before forming a view. Here is my advice as second reviewer.

**Verdict.** Every number in the memo reconciles with the table, script, and figure — crime AMCE ±0.251 corrected with CI [0.168, 0.334]; driving time 0.237 → 0.141 under the 45-minute reference; MM ranges 0.251 / 0.237 / 0.198; bootstrap differences 0.014 (SE 0.056, p = 0.80) and 0.053 (SE 0.055, p = 0.33). The underlying analysis is sound (the tie-removed MM estimator is reproduced correctly, the bootstrap is respondent-clustered with contrasts fixed rather than re-maximized per draw), and the revised claim is the right claim. Nothing in the memo is factually wrong. What's wrong with it is strategic and presentational: it leaves its single strongest argument unstated, it hides the size of the measurement-error correction, and it has one notation hazard. Fix the following before it goes out.

**1. Add the decisive missing argument: no assignment of reference categories can put any rival above crime (must-fix).** The memo concedes "any heuristic that ranks attributes by their largest displayed AMCE is baseline-dependent" and stops there. The data support a much stronger, exactly-on-point rebuttal. A multi-level attribute's largest displayed AMCE, as the baseline varies, runs between its minimax and its full MM range: driving time between 14.1 pp (ref = 45 min) and 23.7 pp, housing cost between 13.7 pp (ref = 30%) and 19.8 pp — all derivable from the MMs already in the table. Crime, being binary, displays 25.1 pp under every configuration. So the reviewer's scenario is real for the rivals (under the 45-minute reference, driving time's displayed maximum falls below housing cost's — the ordering does flip), but crime's top position in the AMCE table is invariant to every possible baseline choice, as a point estimate. Then the concession lands precisely where it belongs: what prevents a strict "crime is first" claim is sampling error (p = 0.80 against the nearest rival), not baseline choice. Two sentences turn "the mechanism is mechanically true" into "the mechanism is mechanically true and provably cannot produce the alleged artifact; the softening we adopt comes from a different source." That is the answer to the reviewer's actual accusation, and the memo currently never states it.

**2. Fix "±25.1 pp" (must-fix).** In "flips the sign while leaving magnitude … identical: ±25.1 pp (95% CI 16.8–33.4)", the ± reads as an uncertainty band, and a signed-both-ways estimate next to a positive-only CI is confusing. Write: "+25.1 pp for the 20%-less-crime level against the 20%-more baseline (95% CI 16.8–33.4), and exactly −25.1 under the reverse reference."

**3. Own the correction's size in the memo itself (must-fix).** The headline 25.1 is the corrected estimate; the uncorrected AMCE is 16.5 pp. The correction rescales by 1/(1−2τ̂) = 1.53 with τ̂ = 0.172 estimated from a single repeated task — the table discloses this, the memo never does, and the memo is what the reviewer reads. Add one sentence: corrected and uncorrected scales differ by a common factor, so the attribute ordering, z statistics, and p-values are identical on either scale (report 16.5/25.1 once), and corrected-scale SEs condition on τ̂. Also state which scale the original manuscript reported — the briefing doesn't say, and if it was uncorrected, the memo currently switches scales silently, which this reviewer would catch.

**4. Reconcile the rounding (minor).** The text gives MMs of 0.626 and 0.374 and calls the range 25.1 pp; a reader subtracting the displayed values gets 25.2. The range is computed before rounding (0.251). Add "(computed from unrounded estimates)" or stop presenting the range as the subtraction of the two displayed MMs.

**5. Carry two bootstrap details into the memo (minor).** State the 2,000 draws, and add the clause that preempts the winner's-curse objection to comparing a 2-level range against 4- and 3-level ranges: the fixed contrasts are each attribute's monotone extremes (10 vs 75 minutes, 15% vs 40% of income), set from the design's natural ordering rather than re-maximized per draw.

**6. Small consistency items (minor).** The figure caption's "multi-level driving-time AMCEs genuinely change" contradicts the memo's own correct reparameterization framing — say "the displayed contrasts change; the underlying contrast set does not." In the replication script, the comment at `script.R:29` claims the seed is declared but "bootstrap/sims unused," while the 2,000-draw bootstrap at `script.R:158` depends on it — fix before materials ship. And split the ~90-word final revised-claim sentence.

**What I would not change.** Testing only the two closest rivals is correct (every lower-ranked attribute is further from crime, so those tests are the binding ones); the MM range with the design-span caveat is the right invariant importance measure; and dropping "drives community choice" is the right call. Do not let anyone argue the strict-first claim back in via a one-sided or adjusted test — p = 0.80 against the nearest rival is not a close call.
