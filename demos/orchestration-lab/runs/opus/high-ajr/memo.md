# Memo — How much weight does the AJR IV headline bear?

**Bottom line.** The headline replicates and survives the two control perturbations. It does **not** survive the two sample perturbations — not because they overturn it, but because in both the instrument stops doing any work.

**Replication (base sample, N = 64).** Settler mortality is a strong instrument for expropriation-risk institutions: the first stage is highly significant (β = −0.61, correctly signed — higher mortality → worse institutions) with F = 22.9, comfortably above the F ≈ 10 rule of thumb. The 2SLS coefficient of log GDP on `avexpr` is 0.94 (SE 0.16), nearly double the OLS 0.52 (SE 0.06). This reproduces AJR's headline, including the feature the manuscript leans on — 2SLS well above OLS. *(2SLS via `AER::ivreg`, so the reported second-stage standard errors are the correct IV standard errors, not the naive two-stage approximation. First-stage F is the partial F on the excluded instrument via `car::linearHypothesis`.)*

**What survives.** Adding absolute latitude, or continent dummies for Africa and Asia, leaves the 2SLS estimate in the 0.84–1.00 band — statistically indistinguishable from the base estimate and still well above OLS (0.43–0.47). The first stage weakens (F = 13.1 with latitude, 11.0 with continents) but stays above the threshold. On these two perturbations the result is genuinely robust.

**What does not survive.** The identifying variation in settler mortality is concentrated in the extreme low-mortality neo-Europes (Australia, Canada, New Zealand, the US). Remove them and the first stage falls to F = 8.6, below the rule of thumb; the 2SLS estimate (1.28) is now contaminated by weak-instrument bias and its standard error is understated. Restrict to Africa alone and the first stage collapses outright: the instrument coefficient (−0.11) is statistically zero, F = 0.3, and the 2SLS "estimate" is 2.40 with SE 3.99 and a 95% CI of [−5.4, 10.2] — no information at all.

**What the manuscript may and may not claim.** It may claim that AJR's headline replicates on the base sample and is robust to latitude and continent controls, on a strong-to-moderate first stage. It may **not** present the drop-neo-Europes or Africa-only 2SLS coefficients as reliable estimates, nor treat the finding as established "even within Africa." Crucially, those two specifications are *silent*, not contradictory: a collapsed first stage neither confirms nor refutes the structural coefficient — it simply cannot identify it. The honest statement is that the result holds where the instrument is strong and is untestable where it is not.

**Method note.** Numbers were independently reproduced from scratch by a second, blind estimation on a different vendor's model (GPT-5.5 via Codex), which agreed with the primary R script. Figures in `robustness-table.md`; reproducible code in `script.R`.
