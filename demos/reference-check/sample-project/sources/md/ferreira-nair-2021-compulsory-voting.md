# Ferreira and Nair (2021) — Does Compulsory Voting Make Better Citizens?

**Citation:** Ferreira, Tomás, and Priya Nair. 2021. "Does Compulsory Voting Make Better Citizens?" *Comparative Democratization Review* 12(3): 455–479.
**Type:** Faithful summary (stands in for a PDF→Markdown conversion)

## What the study does

Exploits the staggered introduction of compulsory-voting rules across Talenian
provinces to estimate effects on (a) turnout and (b) a battery of political-
knowledge items.

## Key findings

- Compulsory voting **raised turnout substantially** (roughly 11 points on
  average).
- Compulsory voting had **no detectable effect on political knowledge**. The
  knowledge battery was flat across the reform.
- The authors conclude that being made to vote changes behavior but **not**
  civic competence.

## Key passages (quotable)

> "Mandatory turnout did not translate into more informed citizens: our
> knowledge index is statistically indistinguishable before and after the
> reform."

> "Whatever compulsory voting does, it does not appear to teach."

## Scope and caveats

- The knowledge null is precisely estimated, not merely underpowered (the
  authors report tight confidence intervals around zero).
