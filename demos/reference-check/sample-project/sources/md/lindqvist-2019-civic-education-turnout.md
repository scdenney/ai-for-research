# Lindqvist (2019) — Civic Education and the Decay of a Turnout Effect

> SYNTHETIC SOURCE for the reference-check demo. Invented for teaching. Not a real paper.

**Citation:** Lindqvist, Mara. 2019. "Civic Education and the Decay of a Turnout Effect." *Journal of Veremian Political Studies* 41(2): 203–228.
**Type:** Faithful summary (stands in for a PDF→Markdown conversion)

## What the study does

A panel study of a 2011 civic-education reform in Veremia, following three
cohorts of first-time voters across four national elections.

## Key findings

- The reform raised youth turnout by **about 3 to 4 percentage points** at the
  first election after exposure.
- The effect **decayed across subsequent elections and was no longer
  statistically distinguishable from zero by the second post-reform election**
  (i.e., it faded within two election cycles).
- The author frames this as evidence that classroom effects are real but not
  self-sustaining without reinforcement.

## Key passages (quotable)

> "The turnout premium among treated cohorts is modest, on the order of three to
> four points, and it does not survive beyond the second election we observe."

> "Civic education appears to start a habit it cannot, on its own, maintain."

## Scope and caveats

- Single country (Veremia); self-reported and validated turnout combined.
- The author explicitly cautions against reading the first-election estimate as
  a permanent effect.
