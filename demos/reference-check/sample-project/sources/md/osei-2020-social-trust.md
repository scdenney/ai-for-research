# Osei (2020) — Social Trust and Turnout: A Cross-National Description

> SYNTHETIC SOURCE for the reference-check demo. Invented for teaching. Not a real paper.

**Bib key:** osei2020
**Citation:** Osei, Daniel. 2020. "Social Trust and Turnout: A Cross-National Description." *Talenian Journal of Comparative Politics* 7(1): 12–39.
**Type:** Faithful summary (stands in for a PDF→Markdown conversion)

## What the study does

A descriptive, cross-national analysis correlating aggregate social-trust scores
with turnout across 38 democracies using survey waves from 2005–2018.

## Key findings

- Countries with higher average social trust **tend to have higher turnout**; the
  bivariate association is positive and moderately strong.
- The paper is **explicitly observational and makes no causal claim**. The author
  repeatedly frames the relationship as a correlation that "invites, but does not
  establish, a causal account."

## Key passages (quotable)

> "We document an association, not a mechanism. Nothing here licenses the
> inference that raising trust would raise turnout."

> "The cross-section cannot separate trust from the many things that travel with
> it, from institutional quality to compulsory-voting rules."

## Scope and caveats

- No identification strategy; the author lists reverse causation and confounding
  as live threats and asks readers not to over-read the correlation.
