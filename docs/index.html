AI for Research

Demos, lecture slides, and how-to guides for working with AI agents and skills, not chatbots, in empirical social science.

For students and for faculty colleagues who want to use this material in their own teaching · companion to the Open Science Skills toolkit · maintained by Steven Denney

Most people meet AI through a chat box, paste in a question, and take what comes back. For research that is the failure mode, not the method. The materials here teach the alternative: structured, inspectable workflows that fetch real sources, check their own output, and report what they could not verify. Everything is meant to be run, copied, adapted for a class, and broken.

Demos run them yourself

Small, self-contained projects you can clone and run end to end. Each ships sample files, the exact prompts, expected output, and a warning about where the human still has to look.

Reference & source-claim checking →

Catch fabricated, malformed, and inconsistent citations, then check whether each cited source actually supports the claim attached to it. Runs offline on a tiny manuscript seeded with planted errors.
